How to Sign "How are you?"

1

Start with the "How" sign

With your dominant hand, form a "C" shape with your fingers. Place your thumb on your chin and your fingers pointing upward. Then pull your hand away from your face in a slight arc motion.

2

Sign "You"

Point your index finger directly at the person you're addressing. Keep your other fingers closed in a fist. The movement is a simple, direct pointing gesture.

3

Complete with facial expression

Raise your eyebrows slightly while signing to indicate you're asking a question. This non-manual marker is essential for proper ASL grammar.

Tips & Common Mistakes

Common Mistake

Don't place your "How" sign too far from your chin. Keep it close for clear communication.

Pro Tip

Remember that facial expressions are just as important as hand movements in ASL. Practice in front of a mirror to see both.
